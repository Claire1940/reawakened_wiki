## Understanding the Core Progression System

Progression in this game is divided into three distinct pillars: Leveling, Ranking, and Class Development. Unlike many other RPGs where level is the only metric of power, your "Rank" (ranging from E to S+) determines the difficulty of content you can access and the quality of rewards you receive.

### Stat Allocation and Milestones

Every time you level up, you receive one stat point. These points are the lifeblood of your build freedom. You can distribute them across four primary attributes. Reaching a milestone of 100 points in a specific stat unlocks unique passive perks that can define your playstyle.

| Stat | Primary Benefit | 100-Point Perk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Agility** | Increases movement speed and dash distance. | **Teleport:** Unlocks a high-speed blink ability. |
| **Strength** | Increases raw physical damage and M1 power. | **Posture Break:** Deals significantly more posture damage. |
| **Vitality** | Increases maximum Health Points (HP). | **Resilience:** Passive health regeneration boost. |
| **Intelligence** | Increases Mana (MP) and skill damage. | **Efficiency:** Reduces skill cooldowns and mana costs. |

> **💡 Tip:** Avoid spreading your points too thin early on. Focus on reaching 100 in one stat first to unlock its game-changing perk before diversifying.

## Choosing Your Class and Path

One of the most innovative features of the game is the separation between your **Class** and your **Path**. While your class dictates your combat skills, your path dictates the type of content you will focus on during your journey.

### The Six Combat Classes
There are six total classes available in the early access version. Each class comes with seven unique skills that are unlocked as you invest class points.

1.  **Warrior:** A balanced front-line fighter with high physical burst.
2.  **Tank:** Focuses on crowd control and absorbing massive damage.
3.  **Assassin:** High mobility and critical hit potential; excels in 1v1.
4.  **Mage:** Long-range elemental attacks with high area-of-effect (AoE).
5.  **Healer:** Essential for group play, providing HP and mana recovery.
6.  **Striker:** A fast-paced brawler focusing on rapid-fire combos.

### The Three Gameplay Paths
At the start of your journey, you must choose a path. This is a separate system from your class and defines your "job" in the world.

| Path | Focus | Key Activities |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Chairman** | PvE & Economy | Leading rescue missions, mining rare ores, and resource management. |
| **Assassin** | PvP & Bounties | Hunting down players via the server contract board for rewards. |
| **Hunter** | Bosses & Subjugation | Taking on high-rank boss missions and clearing elite gates. |

## Questing and Gate Mechanics

To grow stronger, you must interact with the Quest Board. Missions are ranked from E to S, and the variety of objectives ensures that the gameplay loop remains fresh.

### Mission Types
*   **Investigate:** A non-combat focused mission where you search for clues and hidden items on the ground.
*   **Clear Gate:** The classic dungeon experience. You must enter a portal, defeat all mobs, and slay the boss to close the gate.
*   **Subjugation:** You are teleported to a specific arena to fight one or two powerful enemies.

### The Gate System
Gates are the primary way to earn high-tier loot and blueprints. There are eight different gate ranks, including special **Red Gates** and **Secret Dungeons**. To access a Secret Dungeon, you need a **Secret Key**, which can be obtained by completing your daily quests or as a rare drop from Red Gates.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If you enter a gate solo, be prepared for a difficulty spike. Bosses have parry mechanics and high-damage AoE attacks that can deplete your lives quickly.

## Daily Training: The Path to Greatness

Following the "Solo Leveling" inspiration, the game features a daily quest system that is more interactive than simple button-mashing. You must complete four physical tasks every day to avoid penalties and earn a massive experience boost or a Secret Key.

*   **Push-ups:** A rhythmic mini-game.
*   **Sit-ups:** Requires timing-based clicks within a moving bar.
*   **Squats:** Similar to sit-ups, testing your reaction speed.
*   **Running:** You must cover a specific distance (e.g., 400 meters) within the game world.

Completing these tasks is the fastest way to gain five levels instantly in the early game. For more information on official updates, keep an eye on the [Roblox Group](https://www.roblox.com/groups/1) for community events.

## Gear, Crafting, and Traits

As you progress through this **reawakened early access guide**, you will realize that raw stats aren't enough. You need equipment that complements your class. Armor and weapons are often dropped as **Blueprints**, which must be crafted using materials gathered from gates (like antennas, spider silk, or mana crystals).

### Traits: The Hidden Power
Traits act as secondary powers or passive buffs. There are currently seven traits in the game. One of the most sought-after is the **Goliath** trait, which provides a massive boost to physical size and damage resistance. Traits are randomized, so you may need to reroll or grind specific content to find the one that fits your build.

| Feature | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Blueprints** | Found in dungeon chests; requires specific materials to craft. |
| **Mining** | Use a pickaxe inside gates to gather ores for gear upgrades. |
| **Rank Evaluation** | Visit the Rank NPC to test your power and officially upgrade your rank. |

## Combat Tips for Early Access

1.  **Master the Dash:** Use 'Q' to dash. Most boss attacks have a visible "telegraph" (a red zone or a specific animation). Dashing at the last second can save your life.
2.  **Posture Management:** Both you and the enemies have a posture bar. If you deal enough damage quickly, you can "break" an enemy's posture, stunning them for several seconds.
3.  **Use Your Pickaxe:** Even if you aren't a Chairman, keep a pickaxe. Mining mana crystals inside gates is one of the most consistent ways to earn currency and crafting materials.
4.  **Healing is Limited:** Unlike other games where you can spam potions, healing in a gate is difficult. Invest a few points in Vitality early on to give yourself a buffer.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get a weapon in the Reawakened early access guide?**

A: You typically start with your fists. To get your first class-specific weapon (like a dagger for Assassins), you usually need to invest at least 5 points into your chosen Class tree or craft a basic weapon from a blueprint found in an E-rank gate.

**Q: What happens if I fail my daily quests?**

A: Failing to complete your daily training can result in a "penalty." In many solo-leveling-style games, this means being transported to a high-difficulty survival zone where you must outlast waves of enemies to escape.

**Q: Can I change my class or path later?**

A: Currently, your path is a significant choice made early on. While there may be "Reset" items available in the shop or through rare drops, it is best to choose a path that aligns with your preferred gameplay style (PvE vs PvP) from the start.

**Q: How do I increase my Rank from E to S?**

A: You must visit the Rank Evaluation NPC. They will assess your current stats and level to determine if you are ready to rank up. Ranking up is essential because high-rank gear often has a minimum rank requirement to equip.
